Risk Register

Key challenges and proposed mitigation strategies.

1. User Engagement/Retention Prob: Med | Imp: High

Users often churn from planning apps if they don't see immediate value or if the process becomes tedious. Sustaining daily or weekly engagement beyond the initial novelty period is critical. If users revert to old habits, the LTV forecasts will not materialize, impacting business viability. This risk is particularly high for subscription models where continuous value delivery is expected.

Mitigation:

  • Proactive Nudges: Implement weekly personalized email summaries with new recipe suggestions and meal plan previews. Utilize push notifications for timely meal prep reminders (e.g., "Time to prep for Tuesday's dinner!").
  • Gamification & Community: Introduce cooking streaks, recipe achievements, or badges for reducing food waste. Develop social sharing features for meal plans and recipes to foster community and positive reinforcement.
  • Continuous Value: Regularly update the recipe database, introduce new AI personalization parameters, and integrate new features like seasonal meal plans or holiday cooking guides to keep the experience fresh.

2. AI Hallucinations / Bad Recipes Prob: Med | Imp: High

AI, especially large language models, can generate plausible but incorrect or unappetizing recipes. A single bad recipe can lead to user frustration, mistrust, and churn. Inaccurate ingredient quantities, unsafe combinations, or unfeasible cooking instructions could severely damage the product's reputation and user experience, especially given the food waste reduction claim.

Mitigation:

  • Validation Layer: Implement a robust pre- and post-processing layer. This includes a "banned ingredient combinations" list and heuristic checks for plausible quantities and cooking times.
  • Human-in-the-Loop: Initially, manually review all AI-generated recipes (e.g., first 500), then transition to a sampling model (e.g., 10% of new AI recipes) or focus human review on recipes generated for complex dietary needs.
  • User Feedback System: Incorporate a clear "report this recipe" or rating system that flags problematic recipes for immediate review and removal/correction. Prioritize adapting existing, validated recipes from databases over pure generative AI for core meal plans.
  • Safety First: Prioritize safety and common sense over novelty. Consider general liability insurance for product liability concerns, even if low probability.

3. Competitive Pressure / Differentiation Prob: Med | Imp: Med

The meal planning and food tech market is crowded with established players (MyFitnessPal, Mealime, HelloFresh) and new entrants. While SmartPlate has a clear differentiator, competitors could adapt or larger players could integrate similar AI features, eroding the unique value proposition. Marketing and user education will be crucial to highlight SmartPlate's distinct advantages.

Mitigation:

  • Double Down on USP: Continuously innovate and market the AI-powered personalization and waste reduction features. Make these core benefits impossible for competitors to easily replicate without significant investment.
  • Superior UX & Brand Loyalty: Focus on an intuitive, delightful user experience that fosters strong brand loyalty. Rapidly iterate based on user feedback to stay ahead of feature parity attempts.
  • Strategic Partnerships: Explore early partnerships with influencers, nutritionists, or even niche food brands that align with the waste reduction or healthy eating ethos to expand reach and credibility.

4. AI API Cost Escalation Prob: Med | Imp: Med

Reliance on third-party AI APIs (like OpenAI GPT-4) introduces a cost dependency. As user base grows, API costs can scale rapidly, potentially eroding profit margins if not managed effectively. Price changes by API providers or unexpected high usage could make the unit economics unsustainable.

Mitigation:

  • Cost Monitoring & Optimization: Implement robust API usage monitoring. Optimize prompts and requests to minimize token usage. Cache common responses where possible to reduce redundant API calls.
  • Tiered AI Usage: Consider offering different levels of AI-powered personalization based on subscription tiers, allowing free users to have slightly less complex or less frequent AI interactions.
  • Diversification: Explore alternative open-source LLMs or smaller, specialized models for specific tasks (e.g., ingredient parsing) to reduce reliance on a single, expensive provider as the product scales.

5. Data Privacy & Security Prob: Low | Imp: High

Handling sensitive user data like dietary restrictions, health goals, and grocery shopping habits requires stringent data privacy and security measures. A data breach or misuse could lead to severe reputational damage, legal liabilities, and user churn. Compliance with regulations like GDPR or CCPA is crucial.

Mitigation:

  • Privacy by Design: Integrate data privacy and security considerations into every stage of development. Anonymize and aggregate data where possible.
  • Robust Security Practices: Utilize secure development practices, regular security audits, and strong encryption for all user data at rest and in transit. Leverage Supabase's built-in security features effectively.
  • Clear Policies & Consent: Develop clear, transparent privacy policies and terms of service. Obtain explicit user consent for data collection and processing, especially when integrating with third-party services.
