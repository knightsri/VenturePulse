üéØ Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

SmartPlate targets individuals and families overwhelmed by meal planning but committed to healthier, home-cooked meals, seeking efficiency and waste reduction.

Persona 1: "The Time-Strapped Professional"

Demographics: 28-45 years old, urban/suburban, dual-income households or single professionals. Employed full-time in demanding roles (e.g., tech, finance, healthcare). Income $80k-$150k+.
Psychographics: Values efficiency, health, and convenience. Tech-savvy and open to AI solutions. Feels guilty about frequent takeout/delivery. Wants to cook more at home but lacks the time for planning and grocery runs. Environmentally conscious, appreciates waste reduction.
Pain Points: Spends 5+ hours weekly on meal planning/grocery lists. Decision fatigue when choosing meals. Wastes food due to forgotten ingredients. Struggles to maintain healthy eating habits amidst busy schedule.
Decision-Making Criteria: Time-saving features, personalization, ease of use, ability to integrate with existing routines, positive impact on health and budget.

Persona 2: "The Family Meal Manager"

Demographics: 30-50 years old, parents of 1-3 young children. Suburban, often one parent managing primary household duties. Household income $90k-$180k+.
Psychographics: Focused on providing nutritious, varied meals for family. Strives for organized household. Budget-conscious but prioritizes time and health. Struggles with picky eaters and dietary preferences within the family. Wants to reduce food waste for both financial and environmental reasons.
Pain Points: Juggling multiple dietary needs/preferences. Repetitive meal rotation. Significant food waste from unused groceries. Stress of daily "what's for dinner" question. Limited time for elaborate cooking.
Decision-Making Criteria: Family-friendly features, cost-effectiveness (waste reduction), nutritional value, ease of adjusting to family size, ability to share/collaborate on plans.
