‚öñÔ∏è Regulatory & Compliance

For a meal planning application, regulatory and compliance considerations primarily revolve around data privacy, health claims, and food safety.

  • Data Privacy (GDPR, CCPA): Handling user preferences, dietary restrictions, and personal data requires strict adherence to privacy regulations. Implementing robust data encryption, clear consent mechanisms, and transparent privacy policies is crucial. Supabase's built-in security features will assist.
  • Health Claims: While SmartPlate provides nutritional tracking, it must be careful not to make unsubstantiated health claims or offer medical advice. Disclaimers about consulting healthcare professionals for specific dietary needs are essential.
  • Food Safety: Recipes provided should be accurate and safe. Leveraging established databases like Spoonacular and USDA FoodData Central mitigates risk. The "AI Hallucinations" mitigation plan (human review, validation layer) directly addresses this.
  • Allergen Information: Clearly flagging common allergens in recipes and allowing users to filter by allergies is critical for user safety and liability.
  • Payment Processing: Stripe handles PCI compliance for payment data, but SmartPlate must ensure its integration follows best practices.
  • Insurance: General Liability Insurance ($1M coverage) is a proactive measure against potential issues related to recipe accuracy or data breaches.

Given the nature of the application, these are manageable risks with clear mitigation strategies. Prioritizing legal review of terms of service and privacy policy before launch is a key next step.
